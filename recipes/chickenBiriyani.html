Chicken Biriyani like the Nawabs!

Takes you less than an hour

Chicken BiriyaniBiryani in India doesn't just mean biryani. There are variations across the length and breadth of the country. There is Hyderabadi biryani (which is what I'm sharing today) where the biryani has a lot of gravy or masala and is cooked slowly with rice in a sealed pot

Ingredients

For Marinating the Chicken:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3-4 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup fried onions
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For Cooking the Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

For Assembling the Biryani:

  • 1/4 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • A few strands of saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk
  • 1 teaspoon rose water
  • 1 teaspoon kewra water (screwpine water)
  • 1/2 cup fried onions
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

Instructions

1. Marinate the Chicken:

  1. Clean the chicken pieces and pat them dry.
  2. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, fried onions, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, chopped coriander leaves, chopped mint leaves, salt, and lemon juice.
  3. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, mix well, and cover. Marinate for at least 2-3 hours, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

2. Cook the Rice:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot. Add cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and salt.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook the rice until it is 70-80% done (the grains should still be firm when bitten). Drain the rice and set aside.

3. Assemble the Biryani:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot or a large biryani pot, heat ghee.
  2. Add the marinated chicken along with the marinade and spread it evenly at the bottom.
  3. Layer the partially cooked rice evenly over the chicken.
  4. Drizzle the saffron milk, rose water, and kewra water over the rice.
  5. Sprinkle fried onions, fresh coriander leaves, and mint leaves on top.

4. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

  1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid is not tight enough, seal it with dough to prevent steam from escaping.
  2. Place the pot on a griddle or tawa (to prevent direct heat) and cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and cook for another 30-40 minutes. This allows the chicken to cook through and the flavors to meld together.
  4. Once done, remove from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes.

5. Serve:

  1. Gently mix the biryani, taking care not to break the rice grains.
  2. Serve hot with raita, salan (gravy), or any side dish of your choice.
