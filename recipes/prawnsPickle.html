Prawns pickle Andhra Style

Ingredients

  • 500 grams prawns, cleaned and deveined
  • 1/2 cup mustard oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon roasted fenugreek powder
  • 1 tablespoon roasted mustard powder

Instructions

1. Prepare the Prawns:

  1. Clean and devein the prawns thoroughly. Pat them dry with a kitchen towel.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of mustard oil in a pan and sauté the prawns until they are just cooked. Remove and set aside.

2. Prepare the Pickle Masala:

  1. In the same pan, add the remaining mustard oil and heat it until it starts smoking. Let it cool slightly.
  2. Add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and asafoetida to the oil. Allow them to splutter.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Lower the heat and add red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add the sautéed prawns to the masala and mix until they are well coated.

3. Pickling Process:

  1. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool completely.
  2. Once cooled, add lemon juice, vinegar, roasted fenugreek powder, and roasted mustard powder. Mix well.
  3. Transfer the pickle to a sterilized glass jar.

4. Maturing the Pickle:

  1. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dry place for at least 3-4 days to mature.
  2. Shake the jar gently every day to ensure the prawns are well coated with the spices.

5. Serve:

  1. After 3-4 days, the prawns pickle is ready to be served.
  2. Enjoy the pickle with rice, roti, or as a side dish with any meal.
