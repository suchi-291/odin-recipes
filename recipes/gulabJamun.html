Make your occasion a lot more
sweeter with Juicy Gulab Jamun

Ingredients

For the Gulab Jamun:

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1-2 tablespoons milk (as needed)
  • Ghee or oil for frying

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 1/2 cups sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • A few strands of saffron (optional)

Instructions

1. Prepare the Sugar Syrup:

  1. In a pot, combine sugar and water. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add the crushed cardamom pods and bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 8-10 minutes, until the syrup thickens slightly.
  4. Add rose water and saffron, if using. Stir well and keep the syrup warm.

2. Prepare the Gulab Jamun Dough:

  1. In a large bowl, crumble the khoya and ensure there are no lumps.
  2. Add the all-purpose flour and baking powder to the khoya. Mix well.
  3. Add milk gradually and knead into a smooth dough. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.

3. Shape the Gulab Jamun Balls:

  1. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth, round balls. Ensure there are no cracks.

4. Fry the Gulab Jamun:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a deep pan over low to medium heat.
  2. Gently slide the dough balls into the hot oil. Do not overcrowd the pan.
  3. Fry the balls until they turn golden brown, stirring gently to ensure even frying.
  4. Remove the fried balls with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

5. Soak the Gulab Jamun in Sugar Syrup:

  1. While the fried balls are still warm, place them in the warm sugar syrup.
  2. Let them soak for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the syrup and become soft.

6. Serve:

  1. Serve the Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.
  2. Enjoy your delicious homemade Gulab Jamun!
