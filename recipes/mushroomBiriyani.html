Make the most out of the cold monsoon
with warm Mushroom Biriyani

Ingredients

For the Biryani:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 200 grams mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste

For Garnishing:

  • Fried onions
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Fresh mint leaves

Instructions

1. Prepare the Rice:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot. Add a pinch of salt.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70-80% done (the grains should still be firm when bitten).
  4. Drain the rice and set it aside.

2. Prepare the Biryani Masala:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large pan. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamom pods, bay leaf, and cinnamon stick. Sauté for a minute.
  2. Add the sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they release water and become tender.
  7. Lower the heat and add the yogurt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.
  8. Add garam masala powder, fresh coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix well and remove from heat.

3. Layer the Biryani:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of the partially cooked rice.
  2. Add a layer of the mushroom masala over the rice.
  3. Repeat the layers until all the rice and mushroom masala are used up, ending with a layer of rice on top.
  4. Sprinkle fried onions, fresh coriander leaves, and fresh mint leaves on top.

4. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking):

  1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid is not tight enough, seal it with dough to prevent steam from escaping.
  2. Place the pot on a griddle or tawa (to prevent direct heat) and cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and cook for another 25-30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together.
  4. Once done, remove from heat and let it rest for 10 minutes.

5. Serve:

  1. Gently mix the biryani, taking care not to break the rice grains.
  2. Serve hot with raita or any side dish of your choice.
